Crisis Management Workshop

A practical, expert-led program designed to equip participants with the skills and strategies needed to anticipate, respond to, and recover from crises that threaten organizational safety, reputation, and continuity. Blending theory, real-world scenarios, and hands-on exercises.

Workshop Overview

This intensive workshop prepares professionals to navigate the complexities of crises—from natural disasters and security threats to reputational scandals and operational failures. Participants gain a deep understanding of crisis psychology, structured decision-making frameworks, and proven communication strategies to protect people, assets, and organizational reputation.

3-Day Intensive | 5-Day Masterclass | Custom Corporate Programs

Who Should Attend

  • Protocol officers and team leaders
  • Senior and middle management
  • Risk, compliance, and security professionals
  • Event managers and communications staff
  • HR and business continuity leaders

Key Learning Objectives

Understand the nature, psychology, and dynamics of crises and emergencies

Distinguish between crisis management and risk management

Develop and implement crisis management and communication plans

Lead teams and make effective decisions before, during, and after a crisis

Communicate clearly with stakeholders, the public, and media during crises

Manage critical incident stress and support affected individuals

✦ CORE WORKSHOP MODULES ✦

Introduction to Crisis Management

Types of crises (natural, financial, reputational, operational, etc.) • Stages of crisis: pre-crisis, crisis, post-crisis • The crisis lifecycle and early warning indicators.

Psychology and Dynamics of Crisis

Individual and group behaviour in emergencies • Stress reactions and trauma management • Decision-making under pressure • Maintaining team cohesion.

Crisis Leadership and Decision-Making

Roles and responsibilities of crisis leaders • Structured decision-making frameworks • Delegation and command structures • Leading with empathy and authority.

Crisis Communication

Media relations and public messaging • Social media and reputation management • Internal and external stakeholder communication • Spokesperson training and press conference simulations.

Crisis Planning and Response

Developing and testing crisis management plans • Command structures, checklists, and compliance • Scenario-based exercises and tabletop drills.

Recovery and Post-Incident Actions

Business continuity and recovery planning • Lessons learned and continuous improvement • Psychological aftercare and organizational healing.
