Youth Protocol Program

Designed for young people (Ages 13–19) to acquire essential social, communication, and leadership skills — building confidence, self-esteem, and responsible citizenship for academic, professional, and civic life. Interactive, hands-on learning led by protocol experts.

✦ DOWNLOAD PROGRAM BROCHURE ✦

Program Overview

The Youth Protocol Program targets secondary school students, undergraduates, and young professionals. It aims to:

  • Develop appropriate social and communication skills
  • Foster leadership, active citizenship, and community engagement
  • Teach etiquette for diverse social, academic, and work environments
  • Build self-confidence and awareness of how behaviour impacts others

Delivery Format

Intensive Camp: 1–2 weeks full immersion (summer/holiday periods)

Weekend Sessions: Spread over 4–8 weekends (flexible scheduling)

Format: Interactive workshops, role-plays, group projects, mentorship from diplomats and youth leaders.

Group & school programs available
✦ CURRICULUM PILLARS ✦

Social & Communication Etiquette

Proper greetings, introductions, active listening, non-verbal cues, digital etiquette, public speaking basics.

Dining & Public Place Etiquette

Table manners, behaviour in elevators/airports/transport, acts of kindness, etiquette in libraries, museums, sporting events.

Professional Image & Interview Skills

Grooming, dressing for success, first impressions, mock interviews, follow-up strategies, personal branding.

Leadership & Citizenship

Self-leadership, teamwork, active citizenship, community change projects, debates on social issues, civic responsibility.

Discipline & Program Protocols

Program rules, rights & responsibilities, handling rule violations, progressive discipline, creating a safe respectful environment.

Program Outcomes

Confidence in social & professional settings
Effective & respectful communication
Deep understanding of etiquette & protocol
Leadership & teamwork abilities
Positive representation of schools & communities
“Empowering young Nigerians with the tools to thrive as leaders, professionals, and engaged citizens.”
